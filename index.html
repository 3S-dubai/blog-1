How to Start a Business in Dubai in 2026
Step-by-Step Guide

📅 Updated May 2026 🕐 10 min read 📍 United Arab Emirates ✍️ 3S Business Services ✅ Reviewed by a Licensed UAE Business Setup Consultant

Business setup in Dubai has become a top choice for entrepreneurs worldwide. With 0% personal income tax, world-class infrastructure, and a strategic position connecting East and West, thousands of companies complete Dubai company formation every year. Whether you are a freelancer, startup founder, or seasoned investor, this guide walks you through every step to obtain a UAE trade license and start a company in UAE legally and efficiently in 2026.

Choose Your Business Activity

Before anything else, define what your business will do. Dubai's Department of Economy and Tourism lists over 2,000 approved business activities in Dubai. From trading and consulting to manufacturing and media.

Your activity determines which license type you need and which jurisdiction suits you best. Be specific. "IT Services" and "Software Development" are different activities with different implications.

Commercial Professional / Service Industrial Tourism Freelance
Pro Tip: You can add multiple activities to a single license. Each extra activity typically costs AED 100 to 500.
2

Mainland vs Free Zone vs Offshore

This is the most critical decision in your Dubai company registration. Each setup has distinct rules and benefits.

  • Mainland. Trade anywhere in the UAE and internationally. Regulated by the Department of Economy and Tourism.
  • Dubai free zone company. 100% foreign ownership with tax exemptions. Generally restricted to operating within the free zone or internationally. Over 20+ free zones available including DIFC, DMCC, and Dubai South.
  • Offshore. For holding companies and asset protection. Cannot operate physically in the UAE. No visa eligibility.

3

Select a Legal Structure

Your legal structure affects liability, ownership, and how you can raise capital.

  • Sole Establishment. 100% ownership with full personal liability. Best for freelancers and solo professionals.
  • LLC (Limited Liability Company). Most popular for mainland business setup Dubai. Supports 2 to 50 shareholders. Liability is limited to share capital.
  • Free Zone Company. 100% foreign ownership within a designated free zone.
  • Branch of a Foreign Company. Extends an existing foreign company into Dubai. Parent company remains liable.

4

Register Your Trade Name

Your trade name must follow UAE naming rules. It cannot include offensive language, religious references, or names of existing businesses. Check availability and register via the DET portal or your chosen free zone authority.

  • Name must relate to your activity or be a personal name.
  • Abbreviations of personal names are not accepted.
  • Cost: approximately AED 620 to 700 for mainland name registration.
5

Apply for Initial Approval

Initial approval confirms the UAE government has no objection to your business setup. It is done through the DET or free zone authority. This does not allow you to start trading yet. It is a preliminary green light.

Some activities like healthcare, education, and financial services require additional approvals from relevant government bodies before proceeding.

Timeline: Initial approval is usually granted within 1 to 3 business days for standard activities.
6

Find a Business Location

A registered physical address is required to obtain your UAE trade license. Your options include:

  • Physical office. Lease a commercial space and obtain an Ejari (tenancy contract registration).
  • Virtual office or Flexi-desk. Many free zones offer this for remote setups at lower cost.
  • Shared workspace. Co-working spaces like WeWork or free zone hubs are valid registered addresses.
Cost range: Virtual office packages start from AED 5,000 per year. Dedicated offices in prime areas like DIFC or Downtown can exceed AED 100,000 per year.
7

Obtain Your Trade License

With your name registered, structure finalized, and location secured, you can now apply for your official UAE trade license. Submit your application to the DET (mainland) or your free zone authority.

📋 Documents Required for Trade License Application
  • Passport copies of all shareholders and directors.
  • Completed application form (DET or free zone authority).
  • Memorandum of Association — required for LLCs.
  • Tenancy contract or Ejari (registered lease agreement).
  • Initial approval certificate.
  • No Objection Certificate (NOC) — if currently on UAE visa sponsorship.

8

Open a Corporate Bank Account

Opening a UAE corporate bank account is one of the more time-consuming steps. Banks have strict KYC requirements and may take 2 to 6 weeks to process applications.

  • Popular banks: Emirates NBD, Mashreq, ADCB, RAK Bank.
  • Fintech alternatives: Wio Bank, Liv Business, Zand. Faster onboarding.
  • Documents needed: trade license, MOA, shareholder passports, proof of address, and sometimes a business plan.
Tip: Have a clear business plan ready. Banks want to understand your revenue model and transaction types before approving an account.
9

Apply for Residency Visas

Your trade license entitles you to apply for a UAE residency visa for yourself and your employees. The number of visas depends on your license type and office size.

  • Investor or Partner Visa for business owners. Available for 2 or 10 year durations.
  • Employment Visa for hiring staff.
  • Golden Visa UAE for investors above the AED 2M threshold or highly skilled individuals.
  • Process: Medical fitness test, Emirates ID biometrics, then visa stamping.
Cost: Investor visas range from AED 3,000 to 7,000 depending on duration and processing speed.
10

Register for VAT

The UAE introduced 5% VAT in 2018. Your business must complete VAT registration UAE with the Federal Tax Authority if annual taxable turnover exceeds AED 375,000. Voluntary registration is allowed from AED 187,500.

  • Register via the FTA's EmaraTax portal.
  • File quarterly VAT returns.
  • Maintain financial records for at least 5 years.
  • Corporate Tax at 9% applies to profits exceeding AED 375,000 from 2023 onwards.
